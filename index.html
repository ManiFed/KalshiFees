Methodology

How these fee estimates are built

Every number on this page comes from scripts/kalshi_fee_calculator.py, which pulls public Kalshi market data, applies the exchange's fee formulas era-by-era, and writes kalshi_fee_daily.csv. The dashboard reads that file in the browser and recomputes totals, windows, and charts — no server, no manual editing.

What we are measuring

The calculator estimates fees Kalshi collects from traders — not trading volume, not market cap, and not funding payments on perpetuals. Event-contract fees are reconstructed from candlestick volume; perpetual fees use notional volume against default maker/taker basis-point rates. Funding flows between traders and are excluded.

Two fee eras

Kalshi's fee schedule changed materially through 2025. The script looks up each series' fee type as of the candle's own end timestamp, so a market spanning the boundary gets pre-rollout and post-rollout fees applied separately, and different series can flip on different dates.

Era 1 — quadratic (taker-only)

Only the taker pays. Per contract: ceil(0.07 × mult × P × (1−P), ¢) dollars, where P is the mean traded price and mult is the series' fee multiplier.

Era 2 — quadratic_with_maker_fees

Both sides pay in full on every fill — there is no volume-split assumption. Taker: ceil(0.07 × mult × P × (1−P), ¢). Maker: ceil(0.0175 × mult × P × (1−P), ¢) (maker rate is a fixed 25% of the taker rate). Sports series began rolling onto this fee type from roughly October 2025 onward, per series.

Collector pipeline

  1. Fee multipliers — Fetch GET /series/fee_changes?show_historical=true for each series' multiplier and fee type over time, with GET /series/{ticker} as a fallback for series absent from that log. Zero-fee series (KXBTCY, KXCITRINI, KXDOED) are hardcoded to a zero multiplier.
  2. Market universe — Enumerate open markets via /markets and closed markets via /historical/markets, deduped by ticker.
  3. Candlesticks — Markets open under 24 hours use 1-minute candles; longer-lived markets use daily candles. Both live and historical candle requests include a 6-hour buffer around open/close.
  4. Per-candle fees — Apply the fee formula to each candle's mean traded price and volume using the fee schedule in effect at that candle's timestamp, then bucket into UTC dates and accumulate category columns (cat_*).
  5. Perpetuals — Separate /margin/markets rail; default 5 bps taker / 2 bps maker on notional volume. Funding-rate flows are excluded since they move between traders, not to Kalshi. No daily perp history is available yet, so perp fees are reported as an all-time total rather than folded into the daily series.
  6. Output — Write kalshi_fee_daily.csv and kalshi_fee_monthly.csv; this page loads the daily file.

What the dashboard computes

Given the daily CSV, the browser filters by your selected window, groups by day/week/month, and derives total fees, period averages, a trailing 30-day annualized fee run rate, peak fee day, cumulative fee lines, and category totals. Category columns prefixed with cat_ drive the mix and trend charts.

Known limitations

  • Candle mean price is a midpoint proxy, not fill-price VWAP.
  • Fee rounding uses per-candle granularity, not per-fill — small trades may be slightly undercounted.
  • Series with no entry in the fee-change log default to taker-only before the maker-fee rollout cutoff.
  • Zero-fee or reduced-fee series missing from the API log can bias totals.
  • Rate limits, pagination ceilings, or API gaps can truncate market coverage.
