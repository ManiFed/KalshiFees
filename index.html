Methodology

How these fee estimates are built

Every number on this page comes from scripts/kalshi_fee_calculator.py, which pulls public Kalshi market data, applies the exchange's fee formulas era-by-era, and writes kalshi_fee_daily.csv. The dashboard reads that file in the browser and recomputes totals, windows, and charts — no server, no manual editing.

What we are measuring

The calculator estimates fees Kalshi collects from traders — not trading volume, not market cap, and not funding payments on perpetuals. Event-contract fees are reconstructed from candlestick volume; perpetual fees use notional volume against default maker/taker basis-point rates. Funding flows between traders and are excluded.

Two fee eras

Kalshi's fee schedule changed materially in 2025. The script assigns each candle to an era by its end timestamp, so a market that spans the boundary gets pre-May and post-May fees applied separately.

Era 1 — Taker-only (launch → April 2025)

Only takers paid. Per contract: ceil(0.07 × P × (1−P)) dollars, where P is the contract price.

Era 2 — Taker + maker (May 2025 → present)

Both sides pay. Taker: ceil(taker_mult × P × (1−P)). Maker: ceil(maker_mult × P × (1−P)) with maker_mult = 0.25 × taker_mult. Volume is split 70% taker / 30% maker by default (--taker-fraction).

Collector pipeline

  1. Fee multipliers — Fetch /exchange/series-fee-changes and binary-search the correct taker multiplier for each series at each candle time. Fallbacks cover INX/NASDAQ100 halved rates and zero-fee series (KXBTCY, KXCITRINI, KXDOED).
  2. Market universe — Enumerate open, active, and historical markets via /markets and /historical/markets.
  3. Candlesticks — Markets under 24 hours use 1-minute candles; longer markets use daily candles. Historical pulls include a 6-hour buffer around open/close.
  4. Per-candle fees — Multiply rounded per-contract fees by candle volume, bucket into UTC dates, and accumulate category columns (cat_*).
  5. Perpetuals — Separate /margin/ rail; default 5 bps taker / 2 bps maker on notional. No daily perp history means perp totals may appear as an all-time adjustment outside the daily series.
  6. Output — Write kalshi_fee_daily.csv and kalshi_fee_monthly.csv; this page loads the daily file.

What the dashboard computes

Given the daily CSV, the browser filters by your selected window, groups by day/week/month, and derives total fees, period averages, a trailing 30-day annualized fee run rate, peak fee day, cumulative fee lines, and category totals. Category columns prefixed with cat_ drive the mix and trend charts.

Known limitations

  • Candle mean price is a midpoint proxy, not fill-price VWAP.
  • Fee rounding uses market-candle granularity, not per-fill — small trades may be slightly undercounted.
  • Maker/taker split is assumed (70/30), not observed from the API.
  • Zero-fee or reduced-fee series missing from the API log can bias totals.
  • Rate limits, pagination ceilings, or API gaps can truncate market coverage.
